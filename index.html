DistroKid Upload Tutorial

How to Upload Your First Song to DistroKid

Uploading music for the first time can feel confusing because DistroKid puts a lot of options on one page. This guide breaks the process down into smaller steps using real screenshots so new artists know what to look for.

Start the guide Jump to steps

Quick Info

Time: 10–15 minutes

Level: Beginner

Best for: First-time artists

Goal: Submit one original song for distribution

Before You Start

Have everything ready first.

The upload process is easier when your files and information are already prepared. This prevents you from rushing and making small mistakes that can delay your release.

Final audio file, preferably WAV or another high-quality format
Cover art as a JPG, square, ideally 3000 × 3000 px
Artist name exactly how you want it shown
Song title with correct spelling and capitalization
Release date or target release window
Genre and language of the song
Songwriter and contributor information
Clean or explicit lyric decision

Note: This guide focuses on uploading an original single. Cover songs and albums may have extra steps.

The Upload Process

Follow the steps in order.

Step 1 of 11

Open your DistroKid dashboard.

Log in to DistroKid. From your music dashboard, look for the Upload music button. This is where a new release begins.

Pro tip: Use an email you check often because DistroKid may send approval or rejection notices.

Step 2 of 11

Choose stores and the number of songs.

DistroKid automatically checks many major stores and streaming services. For a first release, you can usually leave the default stores selected. Under Number of songs, choose 1 song (a single) if you are only uploading one track.

Step 3 of 11

Confirm your artist information.

Select the artist or band name that should be connected to the release. DistroKid may also ask whether the artist already exists on platforms like Spotify, Apple Music, YouTube Music, or Instagram.

Step 4 of 11

Fill in the release details.

Add the release date, record label if you have one, language, and genre. This section matters because it tells stores how to categorize your song.

Step 5 of 11

Upload your cover art and enter the song title.

Upload clear, square artwork. Then type the song title exactly how you want it to appear on streaming platforms. Avoid putting featured artists in the song title unless DistroKid specifically asks for it.

Good: square, clear, original artwork
Avoid: blurry images, screenshots, or stretched designs
Step 6 of 11

Upload your audio file.

Choose the final version of your song. Do not upload a rough mix by accident. A high-quality audio file is best because this is the file that will be delivered to streaming platforms.

Pro tip: Listen to the exact file before uploading it. Check for silence at the beginning, wrong versions, or export mistakes.

Step 7 of 11

Add song details.

Enter songwriter information and answer the questions about explicit lyrics, radio edits, instrumental tracks, and AI-generated music. These choices affect how the song is labeled and delivered.

Step 8 of 11

Set preview and pricing details.

You can let streaming services decide the preview clip start time or choose it yourself. You may also see pricing options for stores like iTunes and Amazon.

Step 9 of 11

Review optional extras.

DistroKid offers extra paid features. Read each one carefully before selecting it. If you are new, you can skip optional add-ons and focus on submitting the release correctly first.

Step 10 of 11

Check the required boxes.

Before submitting, DistroKid asks you to confirm important statements about rights, terms, and store selections. Read these carefully instead of clicking through quickly.

Step 11 of 11

Review and submit your release.

Look over the final preview. Make sure the title, artist name, artwork, release date, and store selections are correct before submitting. Once you submit, DistroKid will begin reviewing and sending your release to the stores you selected.

You did it: Your first release is officially on the way.

Troubleshooting

Common questions

Why is my song not on Spotify yet?

It may still be processing. Releases can take time to be reviewed, approved, and delivered to streaming services.

Why was my artwork rejected?

Artwork can be rejected if it is low quality, not square, too small, or contains things stores do not allow. Check the requirements and upload a clean JPG version.

Can I fix mistakes after submitting?

Some edits may be possible, but it is better to catch mistakes before submitting. Spelling, artist names, and audio files should be reviewed carefully.

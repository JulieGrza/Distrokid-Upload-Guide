DistroKid Upload Tutorial

How to Upload Your First Song to DistroKid

A beginner-friendly walkthrough for independent artists who are ready to release music but do not want to feel lost on the upload page.

Start the guide View checklist

Quick Info

Time: 10–15 minutes

Level: Beginner

Best for: First-time artists

Goal: Submit one song for distribution

Before You Start

Have everything ready first.

Uploading goes smoother when you are not stopping every few minutes to look for your artwork, lyrics, or songwriter information.

Final audio file, preferably WAV or another high-quality format
Cover art as a JPG, square, ideally 3000 × 3000 px
Artist name exactly how you want it shown
Song title with correct spelling and capitalization
Release date or target release window
Genre and language of the song
Songwriter and contributor information
Clean or explicit lyric decision

Note: This guide focuses on uploading an original single. Cover songs and albums may have extra steps.

The Upload Process

Follow the steps in order.

Step 1 of 10

Log in to DistroKid.

Go to DistroKid and sign in to your account. If you do not have an account yet, create one before trying to upload your release.

Screenshot: DistroKid login page

Pro tip: Use an email you check often because DistroKid may send important approval or rejection notices.

Step 2 of 10

Click “Upload Music.”

On your dashboard, choose the upload option. This takes you to the page where you enter the release information, upload audio, and select stores.

Screenshot: Upload Music button
Step 3 of 10

Choose your release type.

Select the option that matches what you are releasing. If you are only uploading one song, choose Single. If you are uploading multiple songs together, choose EP or Album depending on your release.

Screenshot: Single, EP, or Album options
Step 4 of 10

Fill in the release details.

Enter the artist name, song title, release date, genre, and other required information. This is where small spelling mistakes can become a big problem later, so slow down here.

Artist name Song title Release date Genre
Screenshot: Release details section
Step 5 of 10

Upload your cover art.

Your artwork should be clear, square, and high quality. DistroKid lists JPG as the artwork file type, with a minimum of 1000 × 1000 pixels and an ideal size of 3000 × 3000 pixels.

Good: square, clear, original artwork
Avoid: blurry images, screenshots, stretched designs
Screenshot: Cover art upload area
Step 6 of 10

Upload your audio file.

Choose the final version of your song. Do not upload a rough mix by accident. A high-quality audio file is best because your song will be delivered to streaming platforms.

Screenshot: Audio upload area

Pro tip: Listen to the exact file before uploading it. Check for silence at the beginning, wrong versions, or export mistakes.

Step 7 of 10

Add song information.

Complete the required song details, including songwriter credits and whether the song has explicit lyrics. If your lyrics are clean, do not mark the song as explicit by mistake.

Songwriter Explicit lyrics Lyrics Preview clip
Step 8 of 10

Choose stores and streaming services.

DistroKid lets artists choose where the song should be sent. Common platforms include Spotify, Apple Music, iTunes, Instagram/Facebook, TikTok, Amazon Music, and YouTube Music.

SpotifyApple MusicTikTokYouTube MusicAmazon MusicInstagram
Step 9 of 10

Review everything before submitting.

This is the last place to catch mistakes. Read every section out loud if you need to. Make sure the title, artist name, release date, audio, and artwork are correct.

  • Is the artist name spelled exactly right?
  • Is the song title spelled correctly?
  • Is the correct audio file uploaded?
  • Is the cover art clear and square?
  • Is the release date correct?
Step 10 of 10

Submit your release.

After reviewing the upload, submit the release. DistroKid will review it and send it to the stores you selected. It can take several days for releases to be reviewed, approved, and delivered to streaming services.

You did it: Your first release is officially on the way.

Troubleshooting

Common questions

Why is my song not on Spotify yet?

It may still be processing. DistroKid says releases can take several days to be reviewed, approved, and sent to streaming services. Each platform also needs time to ingest the release.

Why was my artwork rejected?

Artwork can run into issues if it is low quality, not square, too small, or not formatted correctly. Check the artwork requirements and upload a clean JPG version.

Can I fix mistakes after submitting?

Some edits may be possible, but it is better to catch mistakes before submitting. Spelling, artist names, and audio files should be reviewed carefully before clicking submit.
